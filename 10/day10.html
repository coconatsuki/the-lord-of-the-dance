The Valley of Steps

The Dance of Arrows

After what felt like an eternity, Jimli finally descends into the Valley of Steps. The biting cold of the mountains is behind him. The ground beneath his boots no longer crunches with snow but feels unsettlingly soft, almost too perfect.The trees here are tall and elegant, their leaves whispering in a language Jimli doesn't understand.

The whole place reeks of elves.

"Bah, what kind of place is this? Not a single decent stone to kick. Feels like I'm walkin' on fancy cake." Jimli mutters to himself as he presses forward, alert to the strange noises echoing from the surrounding forest. Birds? No, something... else. The sound of delicate chimes floats on the breeze, along with a scent that's suspiciously... floral.

Finally, he sees it — Standing in front of a massive stone portal is an elf. Tall, slender, and annoyingly smug.

"Ah, a dwarf. How quaint," the elf sneers, looking down at Jimli as if he were something scraped off his boot. "I see the legends of your kind's persistence aren't exaggerated. But I'm afraid persistence alone won't get you through this gate."

Jimli snorts. "And what, pray tell, will get me through, pointy-ears?" The elf straightens, flicking his long hair behind his shoulder with an air of practiced superiority. "Only those who can master the graceful Dance of Arrows may pass," he says, gesturing to the ground in front of the portal.

Intricate patterns of arrows are carved into the stone, glowing faintly. Jimli glares, his fists clenching at his sides. "Grace... bah! Who needs that? I've got rhythm where it counts!" The elf chuckles softly, clearly unconvinced. "We shall see, dwarf. But don't be too disheartened when you fail —few outsiders, if any, have ever succeeded."

"Well, make some room in yer elvish history books," Jimli mutters. With that, he plants his feet on the first glowing arrow, preparing to follow the rhythm.
